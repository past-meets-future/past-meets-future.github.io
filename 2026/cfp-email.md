## Body

Dear colleagues,

We are excited to bring the third edition of Past Meets Future to ACM HCOMP 2026 and ACM Collective Intelligence 2026, and we would love for you to be part of it. Past Meets Future 2026 is a full-day workshop on human-AI interaction for the digital humanities and cultural heritage, and submissions are now open.

The workshop brings HCI and AI researchers together with the people who care for the world's cultural memory: digital humanities scholars and the GLAM sector (galleries, libraries, archives, and museums). Last year we mapped the field's hardest challenges together. This year we explore solutions together: prototypes, systems, studies, write-ups, and works in progress that move any of those challenges forward. Nothing gets solved in one afternoon, and that is the point. Technologists need the expertise of the humanities, humanities scholarship needs builders and makers, and the best work happens with both in the room.

Whatever your field or career stage, we want to see what you are working on. Researchers, students, and practitioners from HCI, AI, the humanities, and GLAM institutions are all encouraged to submit, whether the work is new or previously published.

Topics include, and are not limited to:

- AI and GLAM alignment
- The cognitive, educational, and social impact of these systems
- Ethics
- Crowdsourcing
- Foundation models and "collections as data"
- Immersive technologies such as AR, VR, and XR
- Accessibility

Not sure whether your work fits? Email us and ask.
